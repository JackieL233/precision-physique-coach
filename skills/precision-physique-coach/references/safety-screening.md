# Safety Screening

## Non-Negotiable Boundaries

Do not diagnose, treat, or rehabilitate medical conditions. Do not promise injury prevention, spot reduction, guaranteed body-composition outcomes, or medically precise calorie needs.

Recommend medical clearance or clinician support when the user reports:

- Chest pain, pressure, radiating pain, fainting, unexplained dizziness, irregular heartbeat, or unusual shortness of breath.
- New neurological symptoms, severe weakness, numbness, loss of coordination, or loss of bowel/bladder control.
- Acute injury with swelling, deformity, inability to bear weight, sharp pain, or rapidly worsening symptoms.
- Known cardiovascular, kidney, metabolic, endocrine, or uncontrolled blood-pressure conditions.
- Pregnancy, postpartum recovery, eating-disorder history, amenorrhea, very low energy availability, or compulsive exercise patterns.
- Planned extreme deficits, rapid weight loss, dehydration practices, or supplement/drug practices outside ordinary nutrition.

## Intake Questions

Ask enough to classify risk before writing intense plans:

- Current age, sex, height, weight, training history, and goal.
- Known diagnoses, medications, surgeries, injuries, current pain, and movement limitations.
- Red-flag symptoms during exercise.
- Recent training load, sleep, stress, and recovery.
- For fat-loss goals: dieting history, binge/restrict patterns, menstrual regularity where relevant, and minimum acceptable pace.

## Conservative Coaching Defaults

- Start below the user's claimed maximum recoverable volume if recent training data is thin.
- Prefer gradual progression, clear stop rules, and technique quality over novelty.
- Use pain rules: 0-2/10 discomfort that does not worsen may be monitored; 3-4/10 requires modification; 5+/10, sharp pain, altered mechanics, swelling, or worsening symptoms requires stopping that movement and seeking qualified help if persistent.
- Avoid maximal testing for beginners, detrained users, people in steep deficits, or anyone with uncertain medical risk.
- Treat minors, older adults, pregnant/postpartum users, and clinical populations as specialist cases requiring conservative plans and appropriate professional oversight.

## Evidence Anchors

- WHO 2020 guidelines: adults should accumulate weekly aerobic activity and muscle-strengthening activity, with some activity better than none.
- ACSM preparticipation screening: consider current activity level, known disease, signs/symptoms, and intended exercise intensity.
- General medical safety guidance consistently treats chest pain, fainting, severe breathlessness, or neurological symptoms during exercise as reasons to stop and seek care.
