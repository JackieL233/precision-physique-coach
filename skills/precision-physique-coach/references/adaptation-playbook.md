# Adaptation Playbook

Use this for check-ins and program updates. Look for the smallest change likely to fix the bottleneck.

## Trend Classification

Classify the last 2-4 weeks:

- **On target:** body trend, measurements, performance, adherence, and recovery match the goal.
- **Under-dosed:** no progress, good recovery, good adherence.
- **Over-fatigued:** performance falling, soreness high, sleep/hunger/stress poor, joints irritated.
- **Under-recovered from life:** stress/sleep/travel/illness explain the trend more than training design.
- **Nutrition mismatch:** weight trend does not match target despite claimed adherence; audit tracking first.
- **Measurement noise:** water, sodium, cycle phase, constipation, travel, creatine, or inconsistent measurement.

## Adjustment Order

1. Confirm adherence and measurement quality.
2. Resolve safety or pain issues.
3. Adjust recovery basics if they are the bottleneck.
4. Adjust calories or expenditure for body-weight trend.
5. Adjust training volume/frequency/exercise choice for performance and muscle targets.
6. Reassess after enough time for signal.

## Fat-Loss Adjustments

- Weight loss on target and performance acceptable: keep plan.
- Loss too slow for 2 weeks with good adherence: reduce 100-250 kcal/day or add 1,500-3,000 steps/day.
- Loss too fast, poor sleep, high hunger, or performance drop: add 100-250 kcal/day or reduce cardio/steps.
- Waist decreasing while scale stalls: keep plan another 1-2 weeks unless deadline requires change.

## Lean-Gain Adjustments

- Scale rising slowly, lifts improving, waist controlled: keep plan.
- No scale or performance gain for 2-3 weeks: add 100-200 kcal/day.
- Waist rising fast with poor lift progress: reduce 100-200 kcal/day and audit food quality/tracking.
- Pump and performance poor: consider more carbs around training or reduce fatigue.

## Training Adjustments

- Target muscle not progressing, recovery good: add 1-2 sets/week or improve exercise selection.
- Target muscle sore for days, performance down: remove 2-4 sets/week or reduce failure exposure.
- Joint pain: change exercise, grip, stance, range, or loading profile; do not simply push through.
- Technique unstable: reduce load, simplify exercise, or add practice frequency.

## Plateau Audit

Ask:

- Is adherence truly consistent, including weekends?
- Is the scale trend based on enough weigh-ins?
- Did sodium/carbs/cycle/travel alter water?
- Did steps or cardio drift down?
- Did training effort drift down?
- Is stress or sleep masking progress?

## Next Mesocycle Decisions

- If progress was strong and fatigue low: continue with small progression.
- If progress was strong but fatigue high: deload, then resume slightly lower volume.
- If progress was weak and recovery good: revise exercise selection or add volume.
- If progress was weak and recovery poor: reduce stressors before adding work.
