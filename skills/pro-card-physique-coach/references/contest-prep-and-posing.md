# Contest Prep and Posing

Use this for bodybuilding contest preparation, stage conditioning, posing, show selection, peak week questions, and post-show recovery.

Contest prep is high stress. Treat aggressive leanness, dehydration, stimulant use, diuretics, binge/restrict patterns, fainting, chest symptoms, severe fatigue, amenorrhea, mood disturbance, or rapid performance collapse as safety concerns requiring conservative action and qualified professional support.

## Prep Readiness

Before starting contest prep, verify:

- Official show date, federation, region, division, class, and rules.
- Current photos in required poses.
- Body weight, waist, estimated body-fat, training performance, and health baseline.
- Consistent food tracking or reliable structured meal adherence.
- Stable sleep, stress management, and training execution.
- No red flags for eating disorder risk or medical instability.

## Prep Timeline

Common contest prep length is often 12-24+ weeks depending on starting body-fat, division, conditioning standard, and rate of loss. Leaner athletes may need slower loss and more careful fatigue management. Higher body-fat athletes may need a longer runway rather than crash dieting.

Use phases:

- Early prep: establish deficit, maintain performance, practice posing, keep volume recoverable.
- Mid prep: adjust calories/cardio from weekly trend, prioritize weak points, monitor fatigue and hunger.
- Late prep: protect muscle, manage stress, increase posing specificity, avoid reckless changes.
- Peak week: conservative refinement only; avoid extreme dehydration or untested manipulation.
- Post-show: recovery nutrition, reduced pressure, training restoration, feedback review.

## Stage Conditioning

Stage conditioning depends on division and judging expectations. Track:

- Weekly average scale weight.
- Waist and key measurements.
- Front/side/back photos in consistent lighting.
- Posing video.
- Training performance and pump quality.
- Hunger, sleep, mood, libido/menstrual status where relevant, soreness, pain.

If scale stalls but visual conditioning improves, do not overreact. If visual progress stalls for multiple weeks with good adherence, adjust calories, cardio, or timeline.

## Posing Practice

Posing is training. Include:

- Daily short practice in late prep; lower frequency in improvement season.
- Mandatory poses for bodybuilding/classic where relevant.
- Transitions, breathing, bracing, facial control, foot pressure, and stage presence.
- Video review from front, side, and back.
- Posing coach referral when stage goals are serious.

Posing should reveal physique strengths and hide weaknesses without causing cramping, pain, or excessive fatigue.

## Peak Week Boundary

Peak week should refine a ready physique, not rescue an unready prep. Avoid:

- Severe dehydration.
- Diuretic protocols.
- Extreme sodium/potassium manipulation.
- New supplements or foods.
- Last-minute crash dieting.
- Unsupervised drug practices.

Use conservative, tested approaches and professional support. If the user asks for dangerous protocols, refuse that part and offer safer tracking, posing, food consistency, and logistics guidance.

## Show Logistics

Remind users to plan:

- Registration, federation membership, official rules, weigh-in/check-in times.
- Suit/trunks, tanning, grooming, pump-up bands, travel, food, water, backstage supplies.
- Posing routine/music where relevant.
- Coach communication and photo/video capture.

## Post-Show Recovery

After the show:

- Avoid uncontrolled rebound and all-or-nothing dieting.
- Restore calories gradually or use a structured recovery plan.
- Reduce cardio if appropriate.
- Rebuild training performance.
- Review judge feedback, stage photos, and weak points.
- Decide whether to enter another show only after health and recovery status are stable.

## Contest Prep Output

Include:

- Show/division assumptions and official rule verification reminder.
- Prep phase and timeline.
- Training emphasis and volume/fatigue controls.
- Nutrition/cardio adjustment rules.
- Posing schedule.
- Check-in metrics.
- Peak-week safety boundary.
- Post-show recovery plan.
