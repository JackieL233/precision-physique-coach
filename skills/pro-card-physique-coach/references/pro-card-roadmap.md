# Pro Card Roadmap

Use this when the user says they want to become an IFBB Pro, earn a pro card, compete seriously, choose a division, or build a multi-year bodybuilding plan.

This skill is not an official IFBB Pro League, NPC, or NPC Worldwide source. Rules, regions, qualification paths, pro-card availability, and division standards change. Always direct the user to verify current official rules and event details with the relevant federation, promoter, and contest website before making decisions.

## Current Public Pathway Anchor

The common public route toward IFBB Pro status is to compete in eligible NPC or NPC Worldwide events and earn a pro card at official pro qualifier events. Exact eligibility, class structure, number of pro cards, region rules, and event requirements vary. Treat this as a planning anchor, not fixed legal or federation advice.

## Division Choice

Help the user identify a likely division:

- Men's Bodybuilding: maximum muscularity, conditioning, symmetry, posing, and overall development.
- Classic Physique: bodybuilding shape with weight/height limits, classic lines, symmetry, posing, and conditioning.
- Men's Physique: V-taper, shoulder/back development, waist control, presentation, and board-short criteria.
- Women's Bodybuilding / Physique / Figure / Wellness / Bikini: division criteria differ substantially; verify official standards and local show rules.

Division selection should consider structure, height/weight, muscle insertions, waist/shoulder/hip shape, current muscularity, conditioning potential, posing aptitude, and personal preference.

## Long-Term Roadmap

Plan in seasons, not random weeks:

1. **Assessment season:** photos, measurements, training age, weak points, division fit, health baseline, schedule realism.
2. **Improvement season:** long hypertrophy phase to add stage-relevant muscle while controlling body-fat gain.
3. **Mini-cut or reset:** optional short fat-loss phase if body-fat or insulin sensitivity/adherence becomes limiting.
4. **Contest prep:** sustained fat-loss phase to stage conditioning while preserving muscle and performance.
5. **Show execution:** posing, presentation, registration logistics, travel, tanning/suit/trunks, check-ins.
6. **Post-show recovery:** reverse/controlled recovery, health markers, food relationship, training performance, next-season audit.

## Pro-Card Planning Questions

Ask:

- Which federation and region are you targeting?
- Which division do you believe fits you and why?
- Have you competed before? Results, feedback, photos, judge comments?
- What is your current stage-lean estimate, body weight, height, and weak points?
- How many years can you invest before expecting national/pro qualifier success?
- Can you support coaching, posing, travel, food, gym, registration, suit/trunks, tanning, and recovery costs?

## Evidence Needed Before Aggressive Prep

Require:

- Stable training log and recovery.
- Recent photos in consistent poses.
- Body-weight trend and waist trend.
- Nutrition adherence history.
- Health screening and red flag review.
- A realistic show date and official event link.

## Common Gaps Between Gym Goal and Pro Goal

- Insufficient total muscularity.
- Weak back width/thickness, delts, legs, arms, or conditioning for the target division.
- Poor posing and presentation.
- Waist control and body-fat management in improvement season.
- Inconsistent logs and no objective progression.
- Unrealistic prep timeline.
- Health, sleep, stress, or food relationship not stable enough for contest prep.

## Drug and Enhancement Boundary

Do not prescribe, cycle, dose, source, or protocol anabolic-androgenic steroids, growth hormone, diuretics, stimulants, thyroid drugs, selective androgen receptor modulators, or other performance-enhancing or weight-cutting drugs. If the user asks, explain that drug decisions require qualified medical oversight and carry serious health, legal, and sport-rule risks. Continue helping with training, nutrition, posing, recovery, and data tracking within safe boundaries.

## Roadmap Output

When building a pro-card roadmap, include:

- Target division and assumptions.
- Current gap analysis.
- Multi-season timeline.
- Improvement-season priorities.
- Contest-prep readiness criteria.
- Posing and presentation plan.
- Data to collect each week/month.
- Official rules/events to verify.
- Safety and professional-support recommendations.
