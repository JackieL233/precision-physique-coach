# Goal Decision System

Use this when the user is unsure, wants many goals at once, or has a deadline.

## Goal Priority Rules

Rank goals by the limiting resource:

- Fat loss needs an energy deficit and enough training to retain muscle.
- Hypertrophy needs progressive training, enough energy/protein, and recovery.
- Strength needs skill practice, load specificity, and fatigue control.
- Recomposition needs patience and high adherence.
- Recovery needs reduced stressors and gradual loading.

If goals conflict, state the tradeoff and choose a primary outcome plus secondary guardrails.

## Body-Composition Goal Selection

Use these defaults:

- Choose fat loss when body-fat is high enough that health, waist, or appearance priority dominates.
- Choose lean gain when the user is already lean, performance is stable, and accepts some scale gain.
- Choose recomposition when beginner, detrained, returning from layoff, higher body-fat, or unwilling to push scale change.
- Choose maintenance when stress is high, adherence is low, injury risk is high, or the user has diet fatigue.

## Deadline Logic

- Flexible deadline: choose the safest sustainable rate.
- Fixed event: work backward from date and decide whether the target is realistic.
- Unrealistic deadline: protect health and performance, propose a smaller milestone.

Never endorse dehydration, crash dieting, stimulant misuse, or extreme restriction.

## Rates and Guardrails

Fat loss:

- Common target: 0.5-1.0 percent body weight per week.
- Leaner or performance-focused users: slower.
- Higher body-fat users may tolerate faster early loss if medically appropriate.

Lean gain:

- Common target: 0.25-0.5 percent body weight per week.
- Advanced lifters often need slower gain.

Recomposition:

- Scale can be stable; use waist, photos, strength, and adherence.

## Phase Selection

- **Foundation phase:** beginner, return-to-training, technique gaps, inconsistent history.
- **Accumulation phase:** build volume and skill, moderate loads, controlled progression.
- **Intensification phase:** lower reps for priority lifts, maintain hypertrophy accessories.
- **Specialization phase:** increase frequency/volume for 1-2 weak points, reduce non-priority volume.
- **Cut phase:** preserve lifting quality, reduce calories/expenditure gradually.
- **Deload phase:** reduce fatigue while retaining movement practice.

## Goal Statement Format

Write:

- Primary goal.
- Secondary guardrails.
- Timeline.
- Measurable targets.
- What would cause a goal change.

Example:

`Primary goal: lose fat for 8 weeks while maintaining squat/bench/RDL performance. Guardrails: no more than 0.8 percent weekly loss, no persistent joint pain, sleep average above 7 hours when possible.`
