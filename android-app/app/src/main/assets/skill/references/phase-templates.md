# Phase Templates

Use these as starting structures. Adjust for intake, equipment, recovery, and response.

## 3-Day Beginner Full Body

Purpose: build technique, consistency, and whole-body hypertrophy.

Weekly structure:

- Day 1: squat pattern, horizontal push, horizontal pull, hinge accessory, delts, abs.
- Day 2: hinge pattern, vertical push, vertical pull, unilateral leg, arms, calves.
- Day 3: leg press/squat variant, incline press, row, leg curl, lateral/rear delts, abs.

Default dose:

- 2-3 sets per exercise.
- 6-15 reps for compounds, 10-20 for isolations.
- 2-4 RIR early, progressing to 1-3 RIR.

## 4-Day Upper/Lower Hypertrophy

Purpose: efficient intermediate hypertrophy.

- Upper A: horizontal push emphasis, row emphasis, lats, side delts, arms.
- Lower A: squat/leg press emphasis, RDL, leg curl, calves, abs.
- Upper B: vertical/incline push, pulldown/pull-up, chest isolation, rear delts, arms.
- Lower B: hinge/hip emphasis, split squat or hack squat, leg extension, calves, abs.

Default dose:

- 8-14 hard sets per major muscle per week.
- 1-3 RIR for most working sets.
- Double progression for accessories and most hypertrophy compounds.

## 5-Day Bodybuilding Split

Purpose: more exercise variety and weak point attention.

- Day 1: Upper strength-biased hypertrophy.
- Day 2: Lower quad emphasis.
- Day 3: Push/chest-delts-triceps.
- Day 4: Pull/back-biceps-rear delts.
- Day 5: Lower posterior chain plus weak points.

Keep overlap controlled. If shoulders or elbows complain, reduce redundant pressing/curling volume.

## 6-Day Push/Pull/Legs Repeat

Purpose: high-frequency bodybuilding for users with strong recovery and schedule control.

- Push A: chest emphasis.
- Pull A: lat emphasis.
- Legs A: quad emphasis.
- Push B: delt/triceps emphasis.
- Pull B: upper-back/rear-delt emphasis.
- Legs B: glute/hamstring emphasis.

Use fewer sets per session than a low-frequency split. Monitor fatigue closely.

## Fat-Loss Mesocycle

Training:

- Keep key lifts stable.
- Use moderate volume and avoid excessive failure.
- Add cardio/steps gradually.

Nutrition:

- 10-20 percent deficit to start.
- High protein, fiber, hydration, meal consistency.

Adjustment:

- Use weekly average body weight.
- Do not add cardio and cut calories aggressively at the same time unless there is a strong reason.

## Lean-Gain Mesocycle

Training:

- Prioritize progressive overload and enough weekly sets.
- Add sets to lagging muscles only when recovery is good.

Nutrition:

- Small surplus.
- Carbohydrates around training if useful.

Adjustment:

- If strength and measurements improve while waist gain is controlled, continue.

## Weak-Point Specialization

Choose 1-2 weak points for 4-8 weeks.

- Add 2-6 weekly sets to priority muscle.
- Increase frequency to 2-4 exposures.
- Put priority work earlier in sessions.
- Reduce non-priority volume enough to recover.

Do not specialize every muscle at once.

## Deload Week

- Reduce weekly sets by 30-60 percent.
- Keep movement patterns.
- Keep reps clean and away from failure.
- Use the week to restore motivation, joints, and performance.
