# Anatomy and Movement

Use anatomy to choose exercises, not to impress the user. Explain only the parts that change the plan.

## Major Muscle Targets

### Chest

- Pectoralis major: horizontal adduction, shoulder flexion contribution, pressing and fly patterns.
- Bias upper chest with incline pressing/fly variations.
- Bias mid/lower chest with flat/decline pressing, dips if shoulders tolerate.

### Back

- Lats: shoulder extension/adduction; pulldowns, pull-ups, rows with elbows closer to torso.
- Upper back: scapular retraction and horizontal pulling; chest-supported rows, machine rows, rear-delt rows.
- Traps/rhomboids: rows, shrugs, carries, deadlift variants depending on goal.
- Spinal erectors: hinges, back extensions, loaded carries; manage fatigue carefully.

### Shoulders

- Front delts: pressing; often already trained heavily.
- Side delts: shoulder abduction; lateral raise variations are usually needed for bodybuilding.
- Rear delts: horizontal abduction/external rotation bias; rear-delt fly, face pull, rear-delt row.

### Arms

- Biceps: elbow flexion and supination; curls with different shoulder positions.
- Brachialis/brachioradialis: hammer curls and neutral-grip curls.
- Triceps: elbow extension; long head benefits from overhead work, lateral/medial heads from pressdowns and presses.

### Legs and Glutes

- Quads: knee extension; squat, leg press, hack squat, split squat, leg extension.
- Hamstrings: hip extension and knee flexion; RDL, good morning, leg curl.
- Glutes: hip extension, abduction, external rotation; hip thrust, squat/hinge patterns, split squat, cable abduction.
- Calves: gastrocnemius crosses knee and ankle; soleus is biased with bent-knee calf work.

### Trunk

- Abs: spinal flexion, posterior pelvic tilt, anti-extension; cable crunch, reverse crunch, ab wheel.
- Obliques: rotation and anti-rotation; Pallof press, side plank, cable chop.

## Movement Pattern Map

- Squat/knee-dominant: quads, glutes, adductors.
- Hinge/hip-dominant: hamstrings, glutes, erectors.
- Horizontal push: chest, front delt, triceps.
- Vertical push: delts, triceps, upper chest depending angle.
- Horizontal pull: upper back, lats, rear delts, biceps.
- Vertical pull: lats, teres major, biceps.
- Loaded carry/bracing: trunk, grip, traps, conditioning.

## Structure and Exercise Fit

Individual anatomy changes exercise feel:

- Long femurs may make back squats feel more hip-dominant; use heel elevation, front squat, safety bar, hack squat, or leg press if quad stimulus is the goal.
- Long arms may increase bench range and shoulder stress; dumbbells, machines, grip changes, or board/pin ranges can help.
- Shoulder discomfort in dips or upright rows is common; substitute pressing/fly/raise patterns that feel stable.
- Hip anatomy affects squat stance; choose stance and toe angle that allow depth without pain or compensation.

## Weak Point Reasoning

For a weak point, ask:

1. Is the muscle receiving enough hard sets?
2. Are exercises actually loading the target through a useful range?
3. Is fatigue from other lifts limiting target quality?
4. Is frequency high enough for skill and stimulus?
5. Is recovery sufficient?

Then adjust one lever: exercise choice, weekly sets, frequency, rep range, effort, or recovery.

## Technique Principles

- Stable setup before high effort.
- Controlled eccentric unless the goal requires otherwise.
- Full useful range of motion without losing position.
- Target muscle tension plus measurable progression.
- Pain-free joints and repeatable reps.
