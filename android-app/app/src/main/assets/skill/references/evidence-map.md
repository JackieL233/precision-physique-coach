# Evidence Map

Use this file to explain what research and professional consensus supports the skill, and where the evidence stops. This skill is evidence-informed, not evidence-perfect. Apply research as a decision framework, then individualize by the user's data, constraints, preferences, safety status, and response over time.

## Evidence Hierarchy Used

- Position stand or guideline: use for broad defaults when authored by professional organizations and based on many studies.
- Systematic review, meta-analysis, or meta-regression: use for training/nutrition variables that have many trials.
- Randomized controlled trial: use for specific interventions, with caution about population, duration, and measurement limits.
- Observational or validation study: use for feasibility, adherence, dietary assessment, and image-assisted estimation limits.
- Expert review: use for bodybuilding-specific integration when direct long-term IFBB-style trials are not available.

Do not present any single paper as universal truth. Bodybuilding outcomes depend on training age, genetics, adherence, sleep, recovery, injury status, equipment, nutrition precision, and time horizon.

## Training Programming

Supported capabilities:

- Mesocycle structure, progressive overload, exercise selection, set/rep targets, proximity to failure, deload criteria, and session-volume tracking.

Key evidence anchors:

- ACSM 2026 position stand on resistance training prescription for muscle function, hypertrophy, and performance in healthy adults. Use it for broad resistance-training defaults and for distinguishing health-oriented recommendations from advanced physique specialization.
- ACSM 2009 progression models position stand. Use it for progression concepts, loading, frequency, and periodized advancement in healthy adults.
- Schoenfeld, Ogborn, and Krieger 2017 systematic review and meta-analysis on weekly resistance-training volume and hypertrophy. Use it to justify tracking weekly hard sets and treating volume as dose-responsive but recovery-limited.
- Resistance-training frequency and load meta-analyses from the Schoenfeld research line. Use them to explain why frequency is often a tool for distributing quality volume rather than magic by itself, and why a range of loads can build muscle when effort and execution are appropriate.

Application in this skill:

- Start with recoverable weekly volume, then adjust from performance, stimulus, pain, and fatigue.
- Prefer exercise substitutions that preserve target muscle, movement pattern, rep range, and fatigue cost.
- Use RIR/RPE to manage proximity to failure without demanding maximal effort every set.

Limits:

- Most trials are shorter than a real multi-year physique transformation.
- Many studies use healthy adults, not elite competitors.
- Hypertrophy measurement methods vary.
- Advanced specialization still requires individual response tracking.

## Nutrition and Body Composition

Supported capabilities:

- Calorie targets, protein ranges, carb/fat allocation, meal timing, supplement caution, fat-loss/lean-gain rates, and adjustment from scale/waist/performance trends.

Key evidence anchors:

- ISSN position stand on protein and exercise. Use it for protein intake ranges and distribution logic for exercising individuals.
- Morton et al. 2018 systematic review, meta-analysis, and meta-regression on protein supplementation with resistance training. Use it to support adequate protein as a major lever, while avoiding exaggerated supplement claims.
- ISSN diets and body composition position stand. Use it for energy balance, adherence, diet-style individualization, and body-composition framing.
- Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics, Dietitians of Canada, and ACSM 2016 position paper on nutrition and athletic performance. Use it for energy availability, carbohydrate support for training, hydration, and recovery nutrition.
- ISSN creatine position stand. Use it for conservative creatine monohydrate guidance and safety caveats.

Application in this skill:

- Treat calorie/macronutrient calculators as starting estimates.
- Adjust from 2-4 weeks of trend data when possible.
- Prioritize protein adequacy, calorie adherence, fiber, hydration, and carb timing around demanding training.
- Treat supplements as optional tools, not the foundation.

Limits:

- Nutrition tracking is error-prone.
- Body-fat estimates are noisy.
- Meal timing has less impact than total intake and adherence for many users.
- Medical nutrition issues require qualified clinicians.

## Recovery, Safety, and Injury-Risk Boundaries

Supported capabilities:

- Safety screening, referral language, pain rules, fatigue management, deloads, return-to-training caution, and conservative handling of red flags.

Key evidence anchors:

- WHO 2020 physical activity and sedentary behaviour guidelines. Use it for public-health baseline activity and the value of aerobic plus muscle-strengthening work.
- ACSM preparticipation health screening update. Use it to avoid unnecessary barriers while still escalating symptoms, unstable disease, or high-risk situations.
- ACSM and sports nutrition position statements. Use them to connect recovery, fueling, hydration, and performance.

Application in this skill:

- Ask about chest pain, fainting, unexplained shortness of breath, uncontrolled conditions, acute injury, and eating-disorder risk before aggressive plans.
- Do not encourage pushing through pain that changes mechanics or increases across sets.
- Use deloads, substitutions, and clinician referral when risk exceeds coaching scope.

Limits:

- A skill cannot diagnose injuries, prescribe physical therapy, or replace medical evaluation.
- Visual form analysis can identify observations, not pathology.

## Contest Preparation and IFBB PRO-Inspired Roadmaps

Supported capabilities:

- Improvement-season planning, contest-prep-inspired conditioning, posing practice, readiness criteria, peak-week boundaries, and post-show recovery.

Key evidence anchors:

- Helms, Aragon, and Fitschen 2014 evidence-based recommendations for natural bodybuilding contest preparation. Use it for integrating calorie deficit, macronutrients, supplementation, and prep caution.
- Official IFBB Pro League, NPC, and NPC Worldwide public resources. Use only for rule verification, divisions, event pathways, and current organizer details.

Application in this skill:

- Treat "I Want to be an IFBB PRO" as an aspirational professional-level physique benchmark unless the user explicitly asks for real competition planning.
- Build multi-season roadmaps: assessment, improvement season, mini-cut/reset, conditioning phase, presentation, recovery, and next audit.
- Keep peak-week advice conservative and refuse dehydration, diuretic, or drug protocols.

Limits:

- This skill is not affiliated with IFBB, NPC, or NPC Worldwide.
- Rules, divisions, and qualification paths change.
- Contest prep has high physiological and psychological stress.
- Enhanced-drug protocols are out of scope.

## Visual Analysis and Food Estimation

Supported capabilities:

- Equipment identification, exercise form analysis, food photo nutrition estimate, portion-size uncertainty, and linked training/nutrition check-ins.

Key evidence anchors:

- Image-assisted dietary assessment reviews and meta-analyses. Use them to support food-photo logging as useful but error-prone.
- Boushey and mobile food record research. Use it for the practicality and limitations of image-based dietary records.
- AI/image-based dietary assessment systematic reviews. Use them to state that image recognition can help identify foods and portions, but current systems still need human confirmation and are not stand-alone clinical nutrition tools.

Application in this skill:

- Ask for labels, weighed portions, plate size, recipe, oils/sauces, and restaurant/menu data when accuracy matters.
- Give food-photo calories/macros as ranges.
- Link visual training evidence with food estimates: performance, pump, pain, stimulus, carbs, calories, recovery, and adherence.

Limits:

- Photos miss hidden oil, sauces, ingredients, portion depth, leftovers, and cooking methods.
- Food-recognition and portion-size estimation can be wrong.
- Image-based estimates do not replace weighed food, labels, recipes, or dietitian care.

## Data Tracking and Adjustment Logic

Supported capabilities:

- Weekly check-ins, trend interpretation, plateaus, hard-set/tonnage summaries, photos, measurements, and small variable changes.

Evidence anchors:

- Training meta-analyses support tracking dose and response, not relying on motivation alone.
- Nutrition validation studies show intake reporting is noisy, so repeated measures and trend checks are necessary.
- Bodybuilding reviews emphasize preserving performance and lean mass during physique phases.

Application in this skill:

- Use weekly average body weight, waist, photos, performance, pain, hunger, sleep, and adherence together.
- Avoid changing many variables at once.
- Treat a single weigh-in, one bad session, or one food photo as weak evidence.

Limits:

- Data quality determines recommendation quality.
- Wearables and logs can be useful but imperfect.
- The skill should explain uncertainty when data are incomplete.

## Source Maintenance Rules

- Prefer PubMed, PMC, official organization pages, consensus statements, and peer-reviewed journals.
- Re-check date-sensitive claims about official contest rules, ACSM updates, and federation pathways.
- Add new evidence only when it changes an actionable recommendation or clarifies a boundary.
- Do not cite influencer content as evidence.
