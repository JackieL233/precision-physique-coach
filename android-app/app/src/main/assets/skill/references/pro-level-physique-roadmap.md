# Pro-Level Physique Roadmap

Use this when the user says "I Want to be an IFBB PRO", wants an IFBB Pro-style physique, wants elite bodybuilding-level structure, or wants a multi-season personalized transformation plan. The phrase is treated as an aspirational image: high standards for muscularity, symmetry, conditioning, presentation, discipline, and data-driven execution. It is not assumed to mean the user is literally trying to earn a professional card unless they explicitly say so.

This skill is not an official IFBB Pro League, NPC, or NPC Worldwide source and does not imply endorsement. If the user does ask about real contests, divisions, or qualification pathways, direct them to verify current official rules and event details with the relevant federation, promoter, and contest website.

## What the Name Means

"I Want to be an IFBB PRO" means:

- Train with professional-level seriousness.
- Build a physique goal with clear standards, not vague motivation.
- Use bodybuilding-informed programming, nutrition, recovery, posing, and tracking.
- Review progress from evidence: photos, measurements, logs, performance, adherence, and recovery.
- Treat the IFBB PRO image as an elite benchmark while keeping the plan personal, realistic, and safe.

## Pro-Level Physique Pillars

- Muscularity: enough size in the right places for the user's structure and goal.
- Symmetry: balanced development between left/right and upper/lower body.
- Proportion: shoulder-to-waist, back width, legs, arms, chest, delts, and weak-point balance.
- Conditioning: appropriate leanness for the user's goal without reckless extremes.
- Presentation: posing, posture, photos, confidence, and the ability to display progress.
- Execution: consistent training quality, nutrition adherence, recovery, and check-ins.

## Division-Inspired Thinking

Use competition divisions as inspiration only when useful:

- Bodybuilding-inspired: maximal muscularity, full-body development, conditioning, and posing.
- Classic Physique-inspired: shape, lines, shoulder-to-waist ratio, legs, arms, and classic presentation.
- Men's Physique-inspired: V-taper, shoulders, back width, waist control, and polished presentation.
- Women's division-inspired goals: bikini, wellness, figure, physique, or bodybuilding standards differ; discuss the user's preferred look and verify official standards only if they plan to compete.

Do not force everyone into a division. Use division language to clarify aesthetic priorities.

## Multi-Season Roadmap

Plan in seasons, not random weeks:

1. **Assessment season:** photos, measurements, training age, weak points, health baseline, schedule realism, equipment, and nutrition history.
2. **Improvement season:** long hypertrophy phase to add muscle while controlling body-fat gain.
3. **Mini-cut or reset:** optional short fat-loss phase if body-fat, adherence, or appetite becomes limiting.
4. **Conditioning phase:** sustained fat loss when the user wants a sharper look or contest-prep-inspired conditioning.
5. **Presentation phase:** posing, posture, photo consistency, stage-inspired confidence, and visual review.
6. **Recovery and audit:** restore performance, review weak points, and plan the next development block.

## Readiness Questions

Ask:

- What does "IFBB PRO" mean to you: size, leanness, symmetry, stage look, discipline, or actual competition?
- Which look inspires you most: bodybuilding, classic, men's physique, wellness, bikini, figure, or another physique?
- What are your current height, weight, waist, photos, training age, and main lifts?
- What are your strongest and weakest body parts?
- How many days per week can you train, and how many years are you willing to build?
- How consistently can you track food, training, sleep, measurements, and photos?

## Evidence Needed

Require:

- Stable training log and recovery markers.
- Recent photos in consistent front/side/back poses.
- Body-weight trend and waist trend.
- Nutrition adherence history.
- Health screening and red flag review.
- Clear aesthetic priorities and realistic timeline.

## Common Gaps Between Gym Goal and Pro-Level Goal

- Insufficient total muscularity.
- Weak back width/thickness, delts, legs, arms, chest, glutes, or conditioning for the desired look.
- Poor posing, posture, or photo consistency.
- Waist control and body-fat management during improvement season.
- Inconsistent logs and no objective progression.
- Unrealistic transformation timeline.
- Health, sleep, stress, or food relationship not stable enough for aggressive conditioning.

## Drug and Enhancement Boundary

Do not prescribe, cycle, dose, source, or protocol anabolic-androgenic steroids, growth hormone, diuretics, stimulants, thyroid drugs, selective androgen receptor modulators, or other performance-enhancing or weight-cutting drugs. If the user asks, explain that drug decisions require qualified medical oversight and carry serious health, legal, and sport-rule risks. Continue helping with training, nutrition, posing, recovery, and data tracking within safe boundaries.

## Roadmap Output

When building an "I Want to be an IFBB PRO" roadmap, include:

- What the phrase means for this user.
- Current physique and habit gap analysis.
- Multi-season timeline.
- Improvement-season priorities.
- Conditioning readiness criteria.
- Posing/presentation plan.
- Data to collect each week/month.
- Safety and professional-support recommendations.
